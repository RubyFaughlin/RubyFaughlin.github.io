Completed University Projects

Design in Timber

Pursuing Design in Timber at university has given me the opporunity to explore the intersection between creativity and functionality whilst providing me with a strong foundation in material science, sustainability and problem-solving.

I am grateful that I was able to experience this unit as perviously, as I previously had no experience in the topic and it has allowed me to develop valuable new skills that I will be able to pass onto my future students.

TECH205 - Design in Timber

LOW STOOL

OBJECTIVE: Design and produce a unique low stool for sale in the Taronga Zoo merchandise shop, inspired by the zoo's natural environment and commitment to conservation.

The design of this stool is based off the rock patterns that form naturally along the rocky coastlines of Australia, and in particular, Wollongong. The stool is designed to tell a story of our ancestors and how we are shaped (like the coastline) by those before us.

TECH205 - Design In Timber

LAMINATED BOARD

OBJECTIVE: Manufacture a timber board, accurately following workshop drawings incorporating hand and machine planing techniques, widening joints, an appropriate edge treatment, and a food-safe finish, and be branded with your maker’s mark.

The design for this product highlights once again, the Australian landscape and in particular, how the water meets the land.

TECH205 - Design In Timber

DECORATIVE VENEER SERVING TRAY

OBJECTIVE: Design and manufacture a decorative serving tray. It must demonstrate the use of Keyed Mitre Joints and decorative timber veneering.

This is my serving tray, it highlights contrast as a design feature which is highly aesthetic and implements various veneering methods to demonstrate my developed skills.

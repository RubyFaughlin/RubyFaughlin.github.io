Completed University Projects

The following are a collection of projects that I have developed throughout my major (Food Technology) and minor (Design in Timber) units.

Overview

Food Technology

Pursuing Food Technology at university has given me the opportunity to explore the science, creativity, and innovation behind food. Through my studies, I’ve developed skills in product design, sustainability, nutrition, and consumer-focused research. These projects reflect not only my knowledge and technical ability, but also my passion for creating meaningful food solutions that respond to real-world needs.

Design In Timber

Pursuing Design in Timber at university has given me the opporunity to explore the intersection between creativity and functionality whilst providing me with a strong foundation in material science, sustainability and problem-solving. I am grateful that I was able to experience this unit as perviously, as I previously had no experience in the topic and it has allowed me to develop valuable new skills that I will be able to pass onto my future students.

Chef Lei Masterclass
ROSE MACAROON WITH RASPBERRY LOLLIPOP

This dessert showcases seven complementary components that highlight floral and berry flavours through layered textures. A delicate pink macaron shell encases a smooth rose-infused white chocolate ganache and a tart raspberry crémeux, while a raspberry gel adds a bright, concentrated acidity. The base of beetroot sponge provides earthiness and colour depth, finished with a glossy red chocolate coating and accented by crisp meringue strips for texture and visual contrast.

May 29, 2025

Unique Low Stool with Hand-Drawn Fabric Design
Final TECH205 Project

OBJECTIVE: Design and produce a unique low stool for sale in the Taronga Zoo merchandise shop, inspired by the zoo's natural environment and commitment to conservation. The design of this stool is based off the rock patterns that form naturally along the rocky coastlines of Australia, and in particular, Wollongong. The stool is designed to tell a story of our ancestors and how we are shaped (like the coastline) by those before us.
